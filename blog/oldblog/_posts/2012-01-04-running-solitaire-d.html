--- layout: post title: Running Solitaire D&D date: '2012-01-04T07:36:00.000-08:00' author: Edward Miller tags: modified_time: '2012-01-04T07:36:06.990-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-650763852144575282.post-3329556909090026338 blogger_orig_url: http://prcadventure.blogspot.com/2012/01/running-solitaire-d.html --- I've decided I'd like to actually use the various 4e Dungeons & Dragons books I brought back out to Boston with me by playing a few solitaire games. The plan is to run as many of the small adventures in the Dungeon Delves book until I die. The book has tactics and monster stats handy for each encounter, so should be easier to run for myself than randomly generating a dungeon myself.

An interesting bit is that I'm too lazy to run umpteen monsters at the same time as a full party of adventurers. So, instead of running 4 player characters, I will be running 2! I'm also too lazy to tweak the encounters every time to account for my tiny party: instead, I'm giving my adventurers two initiatives: this will let them do their thing twice each round. No extra hit points, no extra action points, and half the variety of accessible powers will still make this quite a challenge :)

Last night, I ran two of three encounters in the first level delve: I'll write up the delve when I finish the third boss encounter. So far, it has been narrow victories with clinging-to-life-ed-ness.
